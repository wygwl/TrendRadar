The new "site license" system requires South Korea’s largest chipmakers to seek approval for exact quantities of restricted gear, parts, and materials annually, adding complexity but allowing continued operations at their major Chinese facilities. Industry concerns include potential delays in approvals for urgent shipments not specified in the license, though US officials insist on robust systems to address such issues. The proposal reflects Washington’s desire for greater oversight and control over supplies to prevent technology leakage or equipment misuse, while South Korean officials argue that existing VEU security measures already address these concerns. Discussions remain ongoing, and no final decision has been made.
