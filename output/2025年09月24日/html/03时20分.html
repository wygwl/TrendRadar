【China Seeks to Store Foreign Sovereign Gold to Boost Global Market Role】China is seeking to become a global custodian of foreign sovereign gold reserves to bolster its position in the international bullion market, according to sources familiar with the matter. The People’s Bank of China (PBOC), through the Shanghai Gold Exchange (SGE), has been encouraging central banks from friendly nations to purchase and store gold within China’s borders. This initiative aligns with Beijing’s broader goal of reducing dependence on the US dollar and Western financial hubs such as London and Switzerland. The reserves would be housed in SGE-linked custodian warehouses and consist of newly acquired gold that contributes to foreign countries' reserves, rather than relocating existing stockpiles. While China’s domestic gold market is the largest globally, its reported central bank reserves rank fifth worldwide, far behind the UK’s 5,000 tons stored in the Bank of England’s vaults. However, the PBOC has been on a gold-buying spree for ten consecutive months, and demand from central banks has helped push gold prices to record highs. China has also eased restrictions on gold imports and launched offshore vaults and yuan-denominated gold contracts in Hong Kong to internationalize its currency and increase trading volumes. This move could attract nations looking to secure reserves and mitigate risks of financial isolation, particularly after the US and allies froze Russia’s foreign exchange reserves in 2022.
